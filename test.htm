MCI, D40 and D60 (12 VOLTS) alternator service specification.

 

 

MCI.                Tested at 80°F, 26.5°

Field current             Cold output.                          Hot output.

Voltage            12 V                            14 V                                        14 V

Ampere            8.1 to 8.4 amp.                       95 amp at 1000 rpm                 285 amp at 2500 rpm.

                                                            220 amp at 1500 rpm

 

D40, D60.            Tested at 80°F, 26.5°

Field current             Cold output.                          Hot output.

Voltage            12 V                            14 V                                        14 V

Ampere            8.1 to 8.4 amp.                       80-88 amp at 1000 rpm      300 amps at max rpm.

                                                            215-236 amp at 1500 rpm

Note

MCI and Flyer use the same Delco-Remy alternators, so the specification is the same.
Direct current with a maximum rating of 300 ampere. Depending on the production year, the output current may vary from 270 to 300 ampere.
Approximately 200 ampere is produced at normal engine idle speed.
Engine speed of 1000 rpm is approximately 2750 alternator rpm. Generator drive ratio is 2.75:1.
Operating the alternator at outputs greater than 100 ampere for periods exceeding 15 seconds will cause the alternator for overheat, resulting damage to the winding, diodes and bearings.
Tighten the nut to hold drive gear to alternator rotor shaft to 144-155 ft-lb (198-210N-m).
Check rotor shaft thread for damages first prior to dismantling alternator.
Check mounting threads for damages for 24 volts alternators.
